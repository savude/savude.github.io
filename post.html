So... How did I get my internship?

Posted by Ellen Savude, on August 02, 2018

BUMA is my mother’s company, passion and baby. My involvement with the company therefore started long before I commenced my internship. However the internship provided me with an official framework to be finally tied to the business. I chose this specific internship because after I’ve completed my studies, I’d like to work alongside my mom in the business and this was a perfect opportunity to experience first-hand how the business functions. I was also excited about working in a industry that I didn’t know much about, and receive direct apprenticeship from the various partners, employees and suppliers that BUMA has. For me, working in a completely new industry greatly appealed to me because it would mean that I was adaptable and the skills that I have acquired can be applied in any field. This is the most important skill for me to acquire and this internship was the perfect opportunity to test this.

But how exactly did I acquire my internship?

Two weeks before leaving school, I had no internship, neither did I have any prospects for an internship. I remember feeling apprehensive of what is to come next and I had a chat with my mom that night. She was working on getting a new partnership so that she could expand her range of products, and she could also tap into new markets as a result. As she was explaining, I had so many questions for her, who was this new supplier, what did they offer, what was their value proposition, why them & not their competitors? And she didn’t have the response to all these questions, and she told me as a joke, maybe you can help me find the solutions, and just like that I was hired. Not the most conventional type of interview, but this is how I got my internship two weeks before heading back home.

My Position

I was hired as a Business Development Intern, which was aligned more with the company and its current set of strategies (it’s in its expansion phase), over what I believe I’m more qualified to do, which is in line with my degree. However, as I mentioned previously, I was keen to take on this position because I believe that knowledge/skills do not work in isolation and so I was assured I would apply my learnings somewhere.
